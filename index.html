Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

In this interactive chart you can analyze the correlation between each of three options that each axis has: for x, percentage of poverty population, age or household income; for y, percentage of obese, percentage of population that smokes or that lack healthcare. By clicking on the text you select that specific data.

Age has no correlation with percentage of population that has healthcare program, smokes or suffers obesity.

The percentage of poverty population seems to have a positive, but not strong, correlation with the percentage of population that smokes, lacs healthcare or is obese.

The most notorious correlation is the household income. There is a negative correlation with percentage of population that lacks healthcare. Also, the higher the household income, less percentage of smoke or obese population percentage.
